Common 4‑Speaker Team Structure Universal Basics

  1. Opener (4–6 min): definitions, burdens, roadmap, Argument‑1.
  2. Point‑1 (4–6 min): flagship argument with evidence + weighing.
  3. Point‑2 (4–6 min): second angle, feasibility, comparative.
  4. Closer (3–5 min): collapse, weigh, present voters; no new args.

Timing varies by tournament; adapt to your rulebook. Keep POIs/POCs respectful and brief where allowed.

🗂️
Flowing tip: Divide your notes into columns: their case, our answers, what judges should believe. Update after each speech.

Focus on clarity, weighing, and consistency across speakers.

Core strategies

  • Give fair definitions. Avoid over‑narrow or abusive framing.
  • State win conditions: what must be proven for each side.
  • Offer a simple theme line to unify your team.
  • Claim: your point in one sentence.
  • Warrant: logic/evidence that makes the claim true.
  • Impact: why it matters to people/judges.
  • Press: ask for missing links, definitions, feasibility.
  • Turn: show their argument helps your side.
  • Compare: use probability, magnitude, timeframe.
  • 30‑second hooks: story, statistic, or rhetorical question.
  • Signposting: "First… Second… Therefore…" — help judges flow.
  • Voice: slower for key claims; pause after impacts.
  • Shared doc with definitions, evidence links, and voters.
  • Hand signals for time, speed, and emphasis.
  • Pre‑agree who answers which expected POIs.
  • Give 2–3 reasons to prefer your side, not 10.
  • Always explain why judges should care.
  • End every speech with a crisp one‑line takeaway.
